A: Radial Ring

Assets orbit the depot as dots on a ring. Status = border color. Hover a dot for details, click to fly. Scales visually — ring grows with count. Minimizes to just the center depot dot.

Pros

  • Unique, map-native feel
  • Status visible at a glance
  • No scrollbar needed (≤20 assets)
  • Gorgeous with neon theme

Cons

  • Gets crowded past ~20 assets
  • Needs overflow list for large depots
  • More complex to implement

B: Polished Floating Drawer

Evolution of the current card — but with a proper header, status summary bar, styled rows with status dots and fly-to arrows, custom scroll, and close button. Minimizes to a small pill.

Pros

  • Familiar pattern — easy to use
  • Handles any asset count
  • Easiest to implement
  • Status summary at top

Cons

  • Less visually distinctive
  • Still a scrolling list
  • Takes more vertical space

C: Hybrid — Ring + Side Panel

Radial ring for the visual preview (≤6 dots), connected to a compact side panel that expands for the full list. Best of both worlds — visual impact + scrollable fallback for large depots.

Pros

  • Visual wow factor of the ring
  • Scales to any depot size
  • Ring shows status at a glance
  • Panel handles overflow

Cons

  • Most complex to build
  • Takes more horizontal space
  • Two interaction zones to manage

My recommendation: A (Radial Ring) — it's the most map-native and visually distinctive. For depots with >20 assets, it can gracefully degrade to stacked rings or show a count badge that opens a mini-list. But B is the safest bet if you want something solid fast.
